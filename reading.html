成语故事： 郑人买履 zhèng rén mǎi lǚ

来源：汉语国学网

从前有个郑国人想买一双鞋，就先在家里拿根绳子量好脚的尺寸，然后上街买鞋，他选好一双鞋想比比大小，发现量尺寸的绳子忘在家里，就赶忙回家去拿。等他拿回绳子，集市已经散了。有人说他怎么不用自己的脚试鞋呢，他只相信绳子。

Once upon a time, there was a Zheng Guoren who wanted to buy a pair of shoes, so he first took a rope at home to measure the size of his feet, and then went to the street to buy shoes. By the time he got the rope back, the bazaar had already dispersed. Some people say how he doesn't try shoes with his own feet, he only believes in ropes.

这个故事告诉我们：做事要懂得变通，不能太死板，要从实际出发，尊重客观事实，不能总是认为书本上的才是真理。

This story tells us that we must know how to be flexible in doing things, not too rigid, we must proceed from reality, respect objective facts, and not always think that what is in books is the truth.

成语故事：买椟还珠 mǎi dú huán zhū

来源：汉语国学网

传说楚国有个珠宝商到郑国去卖珍珠，为了吸引顾客，他做一个很漂亮的用名贵木料做成的盒子，匣子还镶嵌着宝石和翡翠，看起来十分华贵。一个郑国的买主抱着珍珠匣子便以高价买下，打开匣子取出珍珠还给楚国商人。

Legend has it that a jeweler from the state of Chu went to Zheng to sell pearls, and in order to attract customers, he made a beautiful box made of precious wood, and the box was also inlaid with precious stones and jadeite, which looked very luxurious. A buyer of Zheng Guo bought the pearl box at a high price, opened the box and took out the pearl and returned it to the merchants of Chu State.

这个故事告诉我们：不要只被表面的东西迷惑，要认清事物的真正价值。

This story tells us: don't be fooled only by superficial things, but recognize the true value of things.


正音：

  • “还”，读作“huán”，不能读作“hái”。
